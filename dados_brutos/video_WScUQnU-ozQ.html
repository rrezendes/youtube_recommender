This video is part of the "Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning for Engineers" course offered at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). This course introduces ML/AI theory and applications that are relevant to engineering, with a focus on practical machine learning for civil engineering. In this lecture, we review best practices in machine learning. We discuss the typical different steps of a machine learning pipeline. We review typical pitfalls in data preparation (randomization, normalization, balancing, etc.). We discuss the balance between accuracy, simplicity, and interpretability. Finally, we discuss how to diagnostic a machine learning model by plotting its learning curve—to determine whether a model is overfitted or underfitted.

Webinar: Thursday May 28th, 2020

Speakers: Daniel Kim, Keeley Takimoto, Aniket Kesari, David Harding, Margo Boenig-Liptsin, Suhem Parack
Host: Steven Nian

A conversation with professors and students about how we can make the classroom more hands-on and interactive with data science.

Data science is a bridge to engage students to apply theoretical knowledge in hands-on learning experiences. Because qualitative analysis is the primary focus in many non-STEM classes, students have limited exposure to quantitative analysis, which is essential to fully grasp complex concepts. By introducing data science into the non-STEM class, students are empowered to think critically to extrapolate insights from real-life data through theoretical frameworks.

Open source education initiatives by large academic institutions, such as UC Berkeley, enable professors without large resources to incorporate data science modules into their classrooms.
